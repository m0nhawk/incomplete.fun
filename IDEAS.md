# Ideas

Notes from the current experiment set: the site already leans toward compact, visual math playgrounds with direct manipulation, small finite examples, and a few dynamical/physical systems. Good additions should reuse that language: sliders, editable seeds, orbit traces, spectra, tilings, and invariants.

## Math experiments

- **Billiards on rational polygons** — unfold trajectories in rectangles, triangles, and regular polygons; show periodic paths, dense paths, caustics, and the reflected tiling generated by the orbit.
- **Geodesic flow on surfaces** — start with flat torus and sphere, then compare hyperbolic disk geodesics; let users drag initial tangent vectors and watch divergence of nearby geodesics.
- **Elliptic curve group law** — draw a real cubic, pick two points, animate line intersection/reflection, show repeated addition, torsion points, and modular reductions over finite fields.
- **Modular forms / fundamental domain viewer** — connect the existing modular and hyperbolic pages with an SL(2,Z) action on the upper half-plane; color by j-invariant or theta-like height functions.
- **Quiver mutation playground** — editable directed graphs with Fomin-Zelevinsky mutation; show finite-type examples and surprising periodic mutation cycles.
- **Coxeter reflection kaleidoscope** — generate Euclidean, spherical, and hyperbolic reflection groups from Coxeter diagrams; visualize mirrors, chambers, and word growth.
- **Discrete exterior calculus on meshes** — paint a scalar field on a triangulation, compute gradient/curl/divergence/Laplacian, and relate to the spectral graph playground.
- **Persistent homology sandbox** — drop points, grow balls, and plot Betti numbers/barcodes as components merge and loops appear; pairs naturally with Voronoi/Delaunay geometry.
- **Circle packing / conformal maps** — iteratively pack circles in planar graphs; expose angle defects and boundary shapes, then compare with complex dynamics.
- **Integer relation finder** — given a drawn/typed decimal or generated constant, run a small PSLQ-style search and visualize candidate formulas as lattice short vectors.
- **Markov numbers and mutation tree** — navigate triples satisfying x²+y²+z²=3xyz; connect mutation, Diophantine approximation, and the continued-fractions page.
- **Young tableaux and growth diagrams** — animate RSK insertion, partitions, hook lengths, and limit shapes; a nice combinatorics complement to groups and symmetry.
- **Non-Euclidean cellular automata** — run Life-like rules on hyperbolic tilings from the hyperbolic experiment; compare growth rates with square-grid Life.
- **Automatic differentiation jets** — extend the dual-numbers page to truncated polynomial jets R[ε]/(εⁿ), showing first through higher derivatives in one pass.
- **p-adic fractal explorer** — render p-adic balls as trees/circles, animate Hensel lifting paths, and compare p-adic closeness with real closeness for the same rationals.

## Physics experiments

- **Double pendulum phase portrait** — interactive initial conditions, energy coloring, Poincaré section, and side-by-side nearby trajectories to show chaos.
- **Hamiltonian playground** — edit a potential V(x,y), then show vector field, conserved energy contours, symplectic Euler vs ordinary Euler drift.
- **Wave equation on strings and membranes** — pluck a 1D string or 2D drum; decompose into Fourier/spectral modes and connect to the Fourier and spectral pages.
- **Quantum particle in a box** — draw a potential well, solve low eigenstates numerically, animate phase/probability density, and show tunneling through barriers.
- **Ising model / phase transition lab** — 2D spin lattice with temperature slider; display magnetization, clusters, correlation length, and critical slowing down.
- **Percolation threshold explorer** — site/bond percolation on square, triangular, and Voronoi graphs; show largest cluster and crossing probability curves.
- **N-body gravity sketcher** — drag masses and velocities, compare stable orbits, slingshots, Lagrange-point configurations, and energy/angular momentum error.
- **Ray optics and caustics** — mirrors/lenses as draggable segments; emit ray bundles, reveal envelopes, focal points, and aberrations.
- **Fermat principle / shortest time paths** — vary refractive-index fields and watch light rays bend; connect Snell's law to geodesics and gradient descent.
- **Reaction-diffusion + domain growth** — extend the Gray-Scott lab with growing/shrinking domains, anisotropic diffusion, and feed/kill phase diagrams.
- **Lattice gas / Boltzmann toy fluid** — particles on a grid with collisions; visualize vortices, pressure, diffusion, and the emergence of continuum behavior.
- **Driven oscillator resonance** — mass-spring-damper controls with frequency sweep, Bode-like amplitude plot, phase lag, and resonance curves.
- **Special relativity spacetime diagram** — draggable events and worldlines; Lorentz transforms, light cones, simultaneity slices, and proper time.
- **Electromagnetic field lines** — place charges/currents, trace field lines and equipotentials, show Gauss-law flux through movable closed curves.
- **Least-action path finder** — compare arbitrary paths with numerically optimized action-minimizing paths for falling, oscillator, and central-force examples.

## Extensions of existing experiments

- Add **shared preset URLs** for every canvas experiment so interesting states can be bookmarked and linked from a future gallery.
- Add **snapshot/export metadata**: title, parameters, seed, and short explanation embedded beside saved SVG/PNG output.
- Build a **cross-links panel**: e.g. Fourier ↔ wave equation, spectral ↔ drums/graphs, hyperbolic ↔ Coxeter/geodesics, p-adic ↔ modular/number theory.
- Add **challenge cards** to each page: “find a primitive root”, “make a glider”, “draw a knot with writhe 0”, “find a saddle”, etc.
- Introduce **random-but-explainable presets**: generate a state, then list the mathematical properties that make it interesting.
