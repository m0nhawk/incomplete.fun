# Ideas

Notes from the current experiment set: the site already leans toward compact, visual math playgrounds with direct manipulation, small finite examples, and a few dynamical/physical systems. Good additions should reuse that language: sliders, editable seeds, orbit traces, spectra, tilings, and invariants.

## Ten more simulation ideas

- **Kicked rotor / standard map** — iterate points on a torus with a kick-strength slider; show invariant curves breaking into chaotic seas, resonance islands, and Lyapunov-color orbit dust.
- **Coupled oscillator synchronization** — arrange pendula/metronomes/phase oscillators on editable graphs; vary coupling and watch Kuramoto order emerge through phase wheels and frequency spectra.
- **Heat flow on drawings** — paint temperature on a grid, graph, or mesh; compare diffusion kernels, boundary conditions, conserved total heat, and eigenmode decay.
- **Magnetic pendulum basins** — drop a pendulum above colored magnets; render attraction basins, trajectory traces, and sensitive dependence near fractal basin boundaries.
- **Soap-film minimal surfaces** — drag wire-frame boundaries and relax a triangular mesh; visualize mean curvature, area descent, catenoids, and Plateau-border failures.
- **Random walk limit lab** — launch many walkers on square, triangular, hex, and graph domains; display hitting probabilities, cover time heatmaps, recurrence, and Brownian scaling.
- **Fourier optics aperture bench** — draw slits/apertures and immediately see far-field diffraction via 2D Fourier magnitude; connect interference fringes to the Fourier sketcher.
- **Celestial mechanics resonance map** — tune two orbiting bodies and reveal mean-motion resonances, orbital precession, stability tongues, and Poincaré section dots.
- **Tensor field line explorer** — place sources/saddles or paint a 2D tensor field; trace principal directions, separatrices, singularities, and glyphs over the canvas.
- **Monte Carlo integration casino** — estimate areas, π, and high-dimensional integrals with random samples; compare plain, stratified, quasi-random, and importance sampling convergence.
